## ğŸ“Š Key Business Insights
- Air pollution exposure in India is **geographically concentrated**, with northern states consistently recording higher AQI levels.
- Pollution severity shows **strong seasonality**, with winter months driving peak exposure and health risk.
- **PM2.5 and PM10** are the dominant contributors to poor air quality across high-risk regions.
- States with high population and vehicle density exhibit **sustained pollution pressure**, indicating long-term mitigation needs.
- Health burden patterns highlight increased vulnerability in densely populated states, reinforcing the need for preventive solutions.

Together, these insights indicate that air purifier demand in India is **region-specific, seasonal, and driven by persistent exposure rather than isolated events**.
